36-Item Short Form Survey Instrument (SF-36)

The questionnaire consists of 11 sections, with results presented as scores on 8 scales, structured so that a higher score indicates better quality of life. The following indicators are quantitatively evaluated:

  1. General Health (GH) - overall health status - the patient's assessment of their current health condition and treatment prospects.
  2. Physical Functioning (PF) - reflects the degree to which health limits the performance of physical activities (self-care, walking, climbing stairs, carrying weights, etc.).
  3. Role-Physical (RP) - impact of physical condition on role functioning (work, performing everyday activities).
  4. Role-Emotional (RE) - impact of emotional state on role functioning, assessing the degree to which emotional state interferes with work or other daily activities (including increased time spent, reduced volume of work completed, decreased quality of performance, etc.).
  5. Social Functioning (SF) - determined by the degree to which physical or emotional condition limits social activity (communication).
  6. Bodily Pain (BP) - pain intensity and its impact on ability to engage in daily activities, including work at home and outside.
  7. Vitality (VT) - vitality (feeling full of energy and strength or, conversely, feeling exhausted).
  8. Mental Health (MH) - self-assessment of mental health, characterizing mood (presence of depression, anxiety, general indicator of positive emotions).
